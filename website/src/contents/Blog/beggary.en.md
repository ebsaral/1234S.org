I reflected a little on begging after hearing a phrase from my mother or from people around me from time to time. Like every parent might say, my mother often repeats the sentence: “I worked so that you would not be dependent on others.” While saying this, she frequently emphasizes that she herself worked for years so as not to be dependent on anyone. For example, she interprets my brother asking people on the street for a cigarette as an act of begging. Interestingly, even asking for a cigarette can be seen as begging.

Begging is defined in the dictionary as asking for alms. The dictionary meaning of alms is: “Money, goods, etc., given to the needy or poor without expecting anything in return.”
